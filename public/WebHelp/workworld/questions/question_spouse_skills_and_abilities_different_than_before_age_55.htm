Question - Spouse Skills and Abilities Different Than Before Age 55

Select “Yes” if your spouse’s work skills and abilities are different now than they were before age 55, or “No” if they are not.

Blind individuals between the ages of 54 and 65 who engage in Substantial Gainful Activity (SGA) do not lose eligibility for Social Security Disability Income (SSDI) as long as their skills and abilities are different from what they were before age 55 or before the onset of blindness, whichever is later.

If work skills are different, then the Extended Period of Eligibility (EPE) will not cease because of SGA.
